Model Calibration & Uncertainty Estimation

난이도 / Difficulty: Intermediate
날짜 / Date: 2026-06-08

내용

모델 캘리브레이션과 불확실성 추정은 예측의 신뢰도를 수치화해 운영 의사결정(알람, A/B 라우팅, 휴먼-인-루프)을 안전하게 만드는 핵심 기술입니다.

작동 원리: 캘리브레이션은 확률 예측 P(y|x)가 실제 빈도와 일치하도록 조정합니다. 전통적 방법으로는 Platt scaling(로지스틱 회귀에 기반), isotonic regression, temperature scaling(신경망에 자주 사용)이 있고, 불확실성 추정은 aleatoric/epistemic 분해(논문: Kendall & Gal 2017), MC Dropout(Gal & Ghahramani 2016), Deep Ensembles(Lakshminarayanan et al. 2017), Bayesian NN, 그리고 베타/디리클레트 기반 방법으로 수행됩니다.
실무 지표: Expected Calibration Error(ECE), Negative Log-Likelihood(NLL), Brier score, 신뢰도 다이어그램을 사용해 검증합니다. 프로덕션에서는 학습 후 검증셋으로 재캘리브레이션하고(절대 트레이닝 데이터로 안함), 모델 업데이트 때마다 재평가합니다.

활용

사례: 온도 스케일링(Guo et al. 2017) 및 scikit-learn의 CalibratedClassifierCV 사용
코드:
# Temperature scaling (PyTorch, 간단버전)
def temperature_scale(model, logits, labels):
  T = torch.tensor(1.0, requires_grad=True)
  loss = lambda: F.cross_entropy(logits / T, labels)
  opt = torch.optim.LBFGS([T])
  opt.step(lambda: (loss(), None))
  return T.item()

사례 사용처: Guo et al. 2017(온도 스케일링), MC Dropout은 Gal&Ghahramani 2016(연구/자율주행·의료 모델 불확실성 연구에 적용), scikit-learn CalibratedClassifierCV (sklearn>=0.24)과 TensorFlow Probability(tfp)에서 분포적 접근 제공.
실무 패턴: 검증셋으로 재캘리브레이션, ECE+NLL 모니터링, 임계값 결정에 불확실성 포함(휴먼-인-루프), 앙상블로 epistemic 감소.

Content

Model calibration and uncertainty estimation quantify how reliable predicted probabilities are, crucial for safe production decisions like alerting, routing, or human-in-the-loop escalation.

How it works: Calibration aligns predicted probabilities P(y|x) with empirical frequencies. Common calibration algorithms include Platt scaling, isotonic regression, and temperature scaling for neural nets. Uncertainty estimation separates aleatoric (data) vs. epistemic (model) uncertainty; methods include MC Dropout (Gal & Ghahramani 2016), Deep Ensembles (Lakshminarayanan et al. 2017), Bayesian NNs and Dirichlet/heteroscedastic models (Kendall & Gal 2017).
Metrics and practice: evaluate with Expected Calibration Error (ECE), Negative Log Likelihood (NLL), Brier score and reliability diagrams. In production, always fit calibrators on a held-out validation set and re-calibrate after model updates; monitor ECE/NLL in CI.

Applications

Case: Temperature scaling (Guo et al. 2017) and scikit-learn calibration; MC Dropout for epistemic uncertainty
Code:
# scikit-learn calibration example (sklearn>=1.0)
from sklearn.linear_model import LogisticRegression
from sklearn.calibration import CalibratedClassifierCV
clf = LogisticRegression().fit(X_train, y_train)
calibrated = CalibratedClassifierCV(clf, method='isotonic').fit(X_val, y_val)
probs = calibrated.predict_proba(X_test)

MC Dropout (PyTorch): run multiple forward passes with dropout enabled (model.train()) and compute mean/std.
Used in: Guo et al. 2017 (calibration study), Gal & Ghahramani 2016 (MC Dropout), Deep Ensembles in research and production ML systems for robust uncertainty. Production patterns: calibrate on separate val set, track ECE/NLL in monitoring, prefer ensembles/MC for epistemic uncertainty when safety-critical.
